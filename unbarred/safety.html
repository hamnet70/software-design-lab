At the heart of the humanities is humanity, and in this class, we want to make sure that you and all of your classmates can access course material and feel safe learning exciting, new skills.

Accessibility

We have built this site with a wide range of learners in mind, making our code friendly to different web browsers, different screen sizes, and different types of computers. We have also made the site easy to read for students with a range of visual needs providing high-contrast colors, lots of white space, and fairly large text. If you have a need that is not met by our site, please let the instructor know so that we can adjust our code to be better for you.

Safety

We believe that in order to learn well, students must take risks and sometimes fail. So, in class, particularly when providing feedback on one another's work, we ask everyone to be respectful of that learning process. When things come easily to you, please offer to help others who are struggling. When things come hard to you, please ask for help. The discussion boards are intended to allow you to help others grow with your insights and observations, so please use them to provide only constructive comments. Anyone who does not use the discussion board in a respectful way will no longer have access to it and may be dropped from this course.
